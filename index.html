About the course

Carbon markets remain one of the most-cost effective ways to address climate change mitigation and contribute to low-carbon development. Since 2004, several companies and enterprises have voluntarily participated in carbon markets, through a mechanism called Voluntary Carbon Market (VCM), to display corporate social responsibility, meet their voluntary climate change targets, address investor demands and prepare for future regulations. Companies with a carbon footprint can buy carbon credits to address the aforementioned issues and voluntarily offset their emissions. Some of the leading buyers in the VCM include, amongst others, Microsoft, Google, Philips, Shell, and HSBC.

With more and more companies committing to carbon neutrality and/or net-zero targets, the market for VCM is expected to grow manifold this decade. The Taskforce on Scaling Voluntary Carbon Markets (TSVCM) estimates that demand for carbon credits could increase by a factor of fifteen or more by 2030 and by a factor of up to 100 by 2050. Overall, the market for carbon credits could be worth upward of $50 billion in 2030.

Till date, entities in India have largely participated in the VCM as sellers. They have registered their projects with global voluntary carbon market standards and sold carbon credits. The expected growth of VCM and the demand for carbon credits in the coming years presents a great opportunity for enterprises to develop VCM projects that will potentially create an additional revenue stream as well as contribute to the national and global climate agenda.

Course Objectives

This e-learning course is targeted at enterprises (start-ups, MSMEs, etc.) that are interested in carbon markets, we anticipate that the emission reduction potential from their industry and/or enterprise, and wish to develop a VCM project. After completing the basic and advanced modules of this course, the participants will:



Understand the global climate policy landscape as it stands in 2022



Know about the market players and market structure of the VCM



Understand the pre-requisites for developing a VCM carbon project



Understand the process and lifecycle of developing a VCM carbon project from ideation to generating carbon credits



Get an idea of the investment required to develop a carbon project

Understand the benefits of participating in the VCM


Global Carbon Market


The global development cooperation project ‘Global Carbon Market (GCM)’ is commissioned by German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Climate Action (BMWK) and implemented by Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) GmbH. The initiative was launched in 2008 with the aim to support partner countries in using flexible mechanisms under the Kyoto Protocol and other types of market-based instruments to combat climate change at national and regional scale. Since 2018, the project is being implemented in India, Chile, Tunisia,and Uganda with a focus on novel forms of cooperation that may arise under Article 6 of the Paris Agreement, the voluntary carbon market and dedicated carbon pricing instruments.

In India, with the support of the Ministry of Environment, Forests and Climate Change (MoEFCC), Government of India, the GCM project promotes carbon markets to reduce emissions and prepares both public and private sector partners for the new generation of carbon market mechanisms. The overarching objective of the project is to enhance the capacities of public and private sector decision makers to use existing and new market-based approaches to support the implementation of climate change mitigation actions in the country. It also provides evidence-based policy advisory through comprehensive, demand driven and sector specific studies and reports. Experiences suggest that capacity building is critical in both public and private sector.



Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) GmbH



Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) GmbH is a federally owned enterprise supporting the German Government in achieving its objectives in the field of international cooperation for sustainable development. For over 60 years, GIZ India has been working on behalf of the Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development (BMZ), the German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Climate Action (BMWK) as well as the German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy (BMWi) as main commissioning parties and together with partners in the country for sustainable economic, ecological, and social development. The core areas of Indo-German development cooperation are Energy, Environment, Climate Change and Biodiversity, Sustainable Urban and Industrial Development as well as Sustainable Economic Development.

In the thematic area of Environment, Climate Change and Biodiversity, GIZ India implements several projects supporting communities, public and private institutions to improve and conserve natural resources, minimise risks from climate change and enhance rural livelihoods in four priority areas: 

  • Climate Change and Circular Economy
  • Agriculture
  • Natural Resource Management and Agroecology; and
  • Biodiversity
